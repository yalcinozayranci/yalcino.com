Stage 01 · Discovery

Researching Excellence.

The Digital Friction.

Despite a premium physical fleet, Miles & Miles' digital presence was causing significant drop-off. Our research revealed that high-net-worth users were abandoning bookings due to cumbersome web forms and a lack of mobile-specific logistics tracking.

📱

Mobile Usage

72% of luxury users attempt to browse fleet availability on mobile devices while traveling.

⚡

Speed Expectation

Target users value time above all else. Current web booking took over 6 minutes to complete.

Key Findings

User Pain Points.

Through interviews and competitive analysis, four critical friction areas emerged that shaped our design direction.

01

Limited Digital Access

No dedicated mobile app. Users were forced to navigate a desktop-optimised website on their phones, leading to poor usability and high bounce rates.

02

Lack of Self-Service

Booking still required phone calls during business hours. High-net-worth clients expected instant, 24/7 digital booking capabilities.

03

Inconvenient Logistics

No real-time tracking for car delivery or pickup. Users had no visibility into where their vehicle was or when it would arrive.

04

Missing Personalisation

The system didn't remember user preferences or past bookings. Every rental felt like a first-time experience rather than a premium relationship.

Stage 05 · Structure

Hierarchy over UI.

Iterating on Clarity.

We focused on a "Mobile-First Utility" approach, validating that car specifications and rental inclusions were visible at a single glance without scrolling.

Layered Architecture

Separating technical data from emotional visuals.

Seamless Transitions

Ensuring zero loading states between car selection and booking details.
